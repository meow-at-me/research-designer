01 Thesis

Speed is the interface for usable 3D.

Reconstructing a scene shouldn't take minutes of per-scene fitting or known camera poses. WhiskerSplat predicts a metric-scale anisotropic-splat field in a single forward pass.

A cross-view attention module resolves scale ambiguity that otherwise needs pose supervision — so three casual photos are enough, and the result is interactive, not a render queue.

02 Why it matters

The interface is feed-forward.

Prior sparse-view methods trade speed for quality or demand poses. WhiskerSplat keeps both — one pass, no poses.

Baseline · slow

Baseline · constrained

04 Findings

What carries the result.

F1

Cross-view attention replaces pose supervision.

Removing the cross-view attention module drops PSNR by 1.31 dB and reintroduces scale drift. Attention across the sparse views recovers metric scale without any camera poses at test time.

F2

One forward pass is ~40× faster than optimization.

At 0.9 s/scene on a single GPU, WhiskerSplat turns reconstruction from a batch job into an interactive one, while matching or beating optimization baselines on quality.

F3

It degrades gracefully from 9 to 2 views.

Accuracy scales smoothly with input count (30.6 → 28.4 → 26.9 dB at 9 / 3 / 2 views), with no retraining — robust indoors and outdoors on CT-Scenes-Hard.

Takeaway

Feed-forward, pose-free, 0.9 s reconstruction is state of the art on CT-Scenes-Hard — fast, efficient, adaptable, powerful, and robust.
