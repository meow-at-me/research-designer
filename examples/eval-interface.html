How do I convert 10 degrees Celsius to Fahrenheit?
Response A
Multiply the Celsius value by 9/5, then add 32. For 10 °C: 10 × 9/5 = 18, and 18 + 32 = 50. So 10 °C equals 50 °F.

A quick mental shortcut: double the Celsius value, subtract 10% of that, then add 32. It lands on the same answer for everyday temperatures.
Response B
You can use the formula F = C + 32, so 10 °C would be 42 °F. Fahrenheit values are always a bit higher than Celsius ones, so this checks out.
