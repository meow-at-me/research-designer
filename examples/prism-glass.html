Frosted glass, over a living light.

Glass only looks real when something moves behind it. Here a slow aurora drifts behind every panel — blurred, saturated, lit on the edges. A fictional demo of the prism-glass theme.

three.js · Motion · Lenis · native scroll-driven

01 — Method

A page that scrolls like a deck.

Titles hold while cards rise into place, then let go as the next section pushes up. The reveal is native CSS scroll-driven motion — compositor-thread smooth, no library.

Surface

Refraction, not paint

This card has no color of its own. The hue is the aurora behind it, blurred and saturated through the glass.

Edge

Lit on the rim

A one-pixel gradient border catches light at the top-left and fades — the detail flat blur always misses.

Frost

Grain on the pane

A faint procedural noise overlay gives the glass a frosted tactility instead of a clean digital wash.

02 — Light

Light that answers the cursor.

Move across the hero panel and a soft highlight follows — Motion springs the light spot toward your pointer. Hover any card and a specular sweep travels its surface.

Motion

Pointer sheen

An animated custom property moves the highlight — registered with @property so it tweens instead of jumping.

Parallax

Depth on scroll

The plate behind this glass drifts at its own speed, tied to scroll progress with Motion's scroll().

Sweep

Specular hover

A diagonal band of light crosses the pane on hover — pure CSS, runs even when scripting is off.
